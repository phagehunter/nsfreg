About this site & its data

An unofficial guide built to make one tedious thing easy: understanding which entities fall under NSF's forthcoming collaboration prohibition, and where the authoritative answers live.

What this site is

When NSF issued its July 8, 2026 Dear Colleague Letter, it pointed the research community at restricted party lists maintained by seven different federal agencies, in seven different formats, on seven different websites. This site collects a snapshot of those lists into one searchable index, explains each list in plain language, and translates the DCL into practical terms for researchers and research administrators.

What this site is not

  • Not an NSF or U.S. government website. It is independently built and maintained, with no affiliation or endorsement.
  • Not a compliance determination. A search hit is a lead to verify; a non-hit is not a clearance.
  • Not current by definition. Every list here is a snapshot (dates below). Several source lists change monthly or more often.
  • Not legal advice. For decisions with consequences, use the official sources, your institution's research security and export control offices, and researchsecurity@nsf.gov.

Methodology & limitations

  • Extraction: structured sources (CSV, eCFR text, DOCX) were parsed directly; PDF and web sources were converted to text and parsed with per-list scripts. All scripts ship in this site's repository (pipeline/) so the extraction is reproducible and auditable, and per-list JSON lives in data/.
  • Known limitations: PDF extraction can drop or garble characters, especially in non-Latin names; alias detection is pattern-based and imperfect; the CBP list includes long-revoked historical orders (shown with status badges); some State Department snapshots predate 2026; and entities are matched across lists by exact normalized name only, so the same organization under different spellings appears as separate results.
  • Search: matching is normalized (case, punctuation, diacritics) substring/token matching over names and aliases, with country, address, and notes as fallback. There is no fuzzy/phonetic matching — another reason a non-hit is not a clearance.

Verify — always

The official search tools and sources, in one place:

  • Consolidated Screening List — official multi-list screening tool (includes fuzzy name search)
  • OFAC Sanctions List Search
  • Per-list official links — see The Lists
  • NSF Research Security · researchsecurity@nsf.gov

Corrections

Spotted an extraction error or a stale snapshot? Submit your feedback here. You can also open an issue or pull request on this site's GitHub repository — the per-list JSON in data/ and parsers in pipeline/ make fixes straightforward.
