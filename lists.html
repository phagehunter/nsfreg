The restricted party lists, one by one

Appendix A of the DCL names lists from seven federal agencies. Here's what each one is, who maintains it, what kinds of entities appear on it, and where to verify the current version. (NSF's appendix names 12 list categories; the State Department publishes its debarred parties as two separate lists, so this site tracks 13.)

One search to rule most of them: the federal government's Consolidated Screening List (CSL) combines several of these lists (Entity List, MEU, Denied Persons, State debarments, nonproliferation sanctions, and more) into one official search tool. Note the CSL does not include every Appendix A list — the 1260H and 1286 lists, the FCC Covered List, the UFLPA Entity List, and CBP's WRO list must be checked separately at the links below.

About this site

An unofficial, community-built guide to help NSF-affiliated researchers and administrators understand and prepare for NSF's forthcoming prohibition on collaborations with restricted entities. Not affiliated with, or endorsed by, the U.S. National Science Foundation or any federal agency. Nothing here is legal advice.

Project led by Curtis Hoffmann.
