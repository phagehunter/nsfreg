Is a collaborator, appointment, or funder on a U.S. restricted party list?

NSF has announced that in FY2027 it will prohibit NSF-funded collaboration with entities on the U.S. restricted party lists in its July 8, 2026 Dear Colleague Letter. Search a snapshot of all of those lists in one place.

How to read results: a match means the name appears in this snapshot of a listed source — it is a signal to investigate, not a legal determination. A non-match is not a clearance: entities operate under translated names, abbreviations, and subsidiaries. For anything consequential, verify at the official source linked on each result, screen the Consolidated Screening List, and involve your institution's research security office.

What's being prohibited?

Once effective, NSF funds may not support collaboration with restricted entities or their employees, and senior/key personnel may not hold appointments with — or receive research support from — a restricted entity for the duration of their NSF award.

About this site
